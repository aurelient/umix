]]> http://umix.fr/beehives-presentation/feed/ 0 Resmini ‘s Interview at Umix http://umix.fr/resmini-s-interview-at-umix/ http://umix.fr/resmini-s-interview-at-umix/#comments Wed, 01 Oct 2014 16:53:03 +0000 http://umix.fr/?p=104 1) Can you introduce yourself and tell us what you are working on now ?

Currently, I’m an assistant professor at Jönköping International Business School, where I teach informatics, mostly in relation to systems thinking, Information Architecture, and UX design. I was trained as an architect, but in the mid-1990′s I moved in to things more connected to the digital world, to the ways people used to work with spaces and information altogether. That led to a PhD (philosophiae doctor), specifically in informatics. In between a couple of things at the university, I created my own company and I have been working as a consult information architect since 1991. My interests largerly center on how we are moving into space where physical and digital blend through different activities. What are digital supplements ? What physical changes have influenced the way we use digital devices? That’s a very interesting field that has been changing in the past five or six years, and that is the core of my research and my teaching. I’m also the co-author with Luca Rosati of Pervasive Information Architecture (2011), and I am the editor for the upcoming Reframing Information Architecture.

2) What did bring you to Umix ?

It was an invitation that I received from Jean-Michel Salaün. In fact, we sort of had a first meeting two years ago, when I took part in a conference that was organized at Lyon 3 on Information Architecture. I and several friends and professionals in the field were there as invited speakers. If I’m here today, it is because we tried to figure out a way to connect what we are doing within the academic and professional community, and what you are doing over here with the Master and everything connected. So it felt like a good moment to be able to kind of see what students are doing, in order to provide feedback and share ideas. It is always an exchange because I can give a bit of experience and ideas, but I also get inspired and even have a sort of reality-check with the things that came out easily, the things that are difficult to understand, etc. So helping out with the event would be my take on why I’m here.

3) Can you tell us about your personal experience with libraries? What do you think of them nowadays ?

According to me, there are two different things: the use of libraries as a teacher and as an information architect, and my personal experience of libraries. They are places I love, because there is always this little bit of serendipity of finding books and then seeing what lies around and what you could find, just following whatever mood you’re having in front of books. But of course, this is very different when it comes to university libraries and what we have been doing with them. A couple years ago, I had the university of Borâs, where I was working at, do a bit of redesign of the physical and digital library : basically, it was the very first professional interaction that I had with trying to figure out the physical spaces of a library. One of the things Information Architecture has is that it has a past in library science. So that means that if you look back at what we call “classic I.A.”, most of the things that P. Morville and L. Rosenfeld wrote in their book (Information Architecture for the World Wide Web) about classification, categorization, labeling, are coming from library science. The idea that you build your information structures, websites or other things comes from its principles. I think that is definitely one of the roots of what we do with Information Architecture, even though today it’s not enough. The idea that the Internet is a vast library is an idea that I would call the past. We are at the point where the Internet is a larger thing : digital-connected information is not just a library, but it’s a playground, a place where you live, a place where you work, a place where you conduct a part of your life.

That is also a kind of duality I have towards libraries as an item. One of the things that I’m really questioning is how would the libraries survive with digital books and the changes that we’re seeing. How is this going to change the way we use them ? I definitely think that we will need to change some of how we design libraries to reflect different things, because one of the things coming out very clearly from this hackathon, is that all of the different groups are trying to bring social aspect into the library, which is definitely a piece that is missing. The libraries are still very much one of those places where the only authoritative voice is the librarian’s, and we’re not used to that anymore. The idea that you’re bringing an external force into such a tightly controlled world is also going change some of how they work.

4) What do you think future libraries will look like ? Do you think there’s a place for a next-gen library ?

I support the idea that the only way that you can imagine the future is by designing it. And that is exactly what participants are trying to do today. All I know is that future libraries are going to be different because you can already see the big change connected to what we call “pervasive Information Architecture” being deployed, touching all different aspects of daily life. Today’s generation does not separate physical and digital worlds like we used to do in the past: it is the same and only world they live in, digital and physical are just two sides of the same coin. They don’t separate them. So, based on that, I am pretty sure there will be great changes made to libraries, but I don’t know what they will be.

5) Do you think that events like Umix can help to bring innovation to libraries ? If yes, how ?

Absolutely. I generally hope that we are going to tackle libraries now and libraries in the future, but we kind of extend our horizons because there is a need for this kind of practical hackathons events to discuss other things : how to change healthcare ? how to change tax-returns for example ? These events are really interesting and not that complicated to set up, even if it takes resources and money. They are actually feasible. And the awareness that you get of issues and problems from participating into this sort of events is incredibly deep, because you have a chance to discuss with other people, and you see what is happening.  Even the idea that you see that the way you approach a problem is one of the possible ways of approaching it, is going to be one big revelation for many. To become aware of having a specific mindset is a huge discovery for people, and that is what that kind of events allows to be.

6) Anything else you want to add about the event ?

It has been so nice so far and I’m really curious about what we’ll get as results at the end. It is going to be interesting to try to bring it up so we will make it continue after the event in some ways.
