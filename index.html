UmiX invites 40 students to rethink the Information Architecture of Libraries. Over the course of a weekend, participants will envision and prototype innovative services for libraries, considering both the digital and physical spaces. Students from Information Architecture and related disciplines (Information Science, Computer Science, Interaction Design, UX, Information Visualization) are invited.

The event will take place during the last week of September in Lyon at ENSSIB, the french national school of libraries. On Thursday afternoon participants will have the opportunity to pitch ideas and build teams. Friday will be dedicated to hacking prototypes and Saturday morning to the presentations of the results. A multi-disciplinary team will support the teams from the beginning to the end of the project, and a jury will attribute two prizes to the best teams. The teams will also be given the opportunity to present their work at the Blend Web Mix in late October and the World IA day in February 2015.

Andrea Resmini from the Jönköping International Business School and president of the Information Architecture Institute, and Michele Mauri from the Density Design lab in Milan will act are our invited jury members. Aurélien Tabard and Benoit Épron will act as local jury members

 

Practicalities : When/Where/Who

Location: Lyon, France at l’École nationale supérieure des sciences de l’information et des bibliothèques (Enssib) in Villeurbane.

Theme: Rethinking the Information Architecture of Libraries

Organizers: Master in Information Architecture (from ENS de Lyon – Université Lyon 1 – Enssib) and European partners.

Participants: Master students in Information Architecture, Interaction Design, Information Science and Computer Science; young professional.
